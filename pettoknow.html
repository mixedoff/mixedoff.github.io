This is
PetToKnow

Breathing life into mid-fidelity wireframes and turning them into a cohesive UI experience

Summary

As part of my 12-week UI training with xLabs' UI Project, I had the opportunity to collaborate with the UX team on the PetToKnow project. They entrusted me with mid-fidelity wireframes, as well as a persona and it became my responsibility to transform them into a vibrant design. I meticulously curated colors, typography, and a UI kit to craft a seamless and visually captivating high-fidelity design. This design was tailored specifically for a basic pet adoption flow, ensuring a consistent and appealing experience across both desktop and mobile platforms.

Wireframes

The mid-fidelity wireframe focuses on the user's experience of browsing pets and initiating the adoption process by scheduling a date with a pet shelter.

Mood

The initial idea that struck me was an 80s sitcom featuring gangster dogs as the main characters...

...but since this app was specifically tailored for a suburban 30s woman, I have settled with a much smoother mood board.

Filter

Although it was not within the original scope, I anticipated that the inclusion of pet pictures would pose a significant challenge in the design of a pet app. My concern stemmed from the expectation that users would upload pictures of varying quality and style to the platform.

I transformed this disadvantage into an advantage by implementing filters.

And ultimately, I settled on applying a subtle "cartoonized" filter.

Colors

I had to let go of my initial choice for secondary and tertiary colors as I couldn't achieve the desired balance with the intended atmosphere. Consequently, I decided to make yellow my primary call-to-action (CTA) color and utilized lighter shades of slightly tinted white, as my secondary palette./p>

Typography

For the logo and pet names, I opted for the "Mali" font, which exudes playfulness. As for other headings and body text, I utilized the "Poppins" font.

UI kit

I have used the “Material 3” UI Kit and adopted (pun intended) to my advantage.

Logo

I designed a sketched-like icon to serve as the logo for PetToKnow...

Background

...and used it's parts as subtle background features for the app.

Flow

The user browsing through pets, choosing one, and booking a date with a pet shelter.
